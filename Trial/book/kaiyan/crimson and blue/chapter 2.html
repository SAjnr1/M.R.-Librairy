Chapter Two


The bartender leans in, "Looks like you've got an admirer." Oh, so I wasn't the only who was thinking that way.


I turn to him with a playful smile on my lips. "Oh? Which one?"


He nods towards him and says, "The guy by the poker table. He's been watching you since you walked in."


I laugh and it's a low, throaty sound. "I'm not surprised. I do make an entrance, don't I?"


The bartender chuckles, and I know I have him and the other guy, exactly where I want them.


I take another sip of my whiskey, letting my gaze drift back to the unknown man. He is talking to one of the dealers, but his eyes keeps flicking back to me. I smile to myself, feeling a sense of satisfaction. The hook is set, now all I have to do is reel him in.


I turn back to the bartender, leaning in really close. "Thanks for the heads up. What's the story with him?"


"That's Mr. Julian, Mr. Braiden's younger step brother. He's like the black sheep in the family because he's never serious, rebellious and a womanizer. Mr. Arnaldo Mariano transferred ownership of Diamante Corporation to Mr Braiden, together with most of his properties.


But he gave Mr Julian the casino out of all his properties to handle because Mr Braiden did not want to be involved in this shady business." He informs me.


I raise an eyebrow, feigning that I am interested. "Arnaldo? As in, Arnaldo Mariano?"


I didn't know Arnaldo had a son apart from Braiden. He probably kept Julian out of the limelight because he's ashamed of him. I'm sure Julian hates Braiden because he's got most of their father's possessions and this would make it easier for me to bring the Marianos down. Nice shot Officer Mariana Alcaroio!


Six years ago, Arnaldo Mariano handed his business over to Braiden because he was diagnosed with stroke. Somehow, I'm getting a feeling that Braiden is not as bad as I thought because he rejected the casino. But who knows? Maybe there are other shady businesses he might be involved in.


Once I gather enough evidence against Arnaldo Mariano and his sons, I will put them behind bars. I will not kill them because it would make me no difference from them. I will make sure Arnaldo suffers to the extent that he would beg to die.


The bartender nods, "The one and only. You know, he's not someone to be messed with."


I laugh. "I'm not looking to mess with anyone, sweetheart. I'm just looking to have a good time." I say.


The bartender smiles and goes to attend to another person.


"Mind if I join you?" Someone asks and I raise my head up to see Julian by my side. He's handsome, looks smart in his tuxedo but looks rowdy. His jet black hair is messy, brown eyes and his nose looks perfectly sculptured.


He's tall and well built. He has two piercings on his upper left eyebrow, piercings on his ears and a faint scar on his right cheek.


"Not at all. I was just starting to get bored." I say.


He smiles back. "I think I can help with that." He says and wink.


"I'm Julian Mariano and I own this casino." He says thinking, that would impress who? Me?


"Nice to meet you. I'm Rosita Sanchez and I'm looking forward to investing in Eclipse." I say.


"The pleasure's all mine, Miss Sanchez. That's a dream you've got there." He laughs and pulls out his phone searching for something.


After a few minutes, he looks at me as if he's found a treasure full of gold. "Oh... Miss Rosita Sanchez, CEO of Crescent in Cebu City." He says and offers me a handshake.


"Nice to meet you once again, Mr. Mariano." I say smiling confidently.


"Did you come all the way from Cebu to Parañaque for the investment only?" He asks me.


"I'm building an annex of Crescent in Makati and I have decided to stay there for a while to check the construction," I tell him.


"You're a very determined business woman Miss Sanchez." He says and smiles at me.


I smile back knowing that my plan had not backfired. Alex had everything covered and whoever that searched for Rosita Sanchez on the web would find out that I'm a billionaire and own a fashion company in Cebu City.


"I'm glad to have you here and you have met the right person." Julian says. He takes my hand and kisses it. Yuck! I feel like throwing up. Anyone close to Arnaldo despises me.


"I would like us to go to somewhere private so we would could discuss about the investment." He says. I know what menlike him want. They just follow everything that's under a skirt. Foolish him. I'm not going to let him have me, not even the slightest bit.


I laugh, letting him take my hand and he leads me to a hallway. There, we walk past a lot of doors. Julian lets go of my hand, and stops infront of a door. He opens it and welcomes me in. He shuts the door behind us and I turn to look at him.


"To avoid disturbance," he says and shrug. I nod but not quite sure of what he said.


This place is sleek and modern, a contrast to the gritty warehouse outside. It's just like the impression you get when an abandoned building looks like a newly built mansion on the inside.


"So Miss Sanchez," he says, his gaze roaming the room. "What do you think?"


I glance around, taking it in. "It's beautiful, nothing like the warehouse "


"That's it Miss Sanchez. This is my personal longue. Please sit." He says gesturing to a round brown polished table at the corner of the room. There are two wooden polished chairs, just like the table, facing each other on either sides. He pulls the chair for me to sit.


"Thank you," I say and his hands brush my back slightly before he also sits. Did he really have to do that.


"I'm glad to have you here. You're interested in investing in Eclipse?" He asks me.


I lean in and say to him. "I'm looking to diversify my portfolio. I've heard Eclipse is the place to be. It's exclusive, high-stakes, and with a... certain reputation."


Julian chuckles and releases hand. "You've done your research. But what makes you think Eclipse is the right fit for Crescent's interests?"


"I think... the right people, in the right place, can make all the difference. I'm looking for opportunities that align with my... risk appetite." I say.


Julian's smile widens and I can tell how clearly intrigued he is. "Risk appetite, I like that. And you're right, Eclipse is a place where... high rollers play. But it's not just about the money. What do you know about our operations here?"


"I know enough to know I want to partner with you. I've heard rumors about the kind of clientele you attract, the... discretion you offer." I say.


"Discretion is key here, Miss Sanchez. We value our patrons' privacy. But I have to ask, what makes you think you can handle the... unique environment of Eclipse?" He asks me.


"I think I've handled tougher crowds than this, Mr. Mariano. And I'm not just talking about fashion shows." I tell him and he smiles.


"I'm starting to think you're more than just a pretty face, Miss Sanchez. Alright, let's discuss the details of your investment." Julian says.


Julian steps closer and his voice is husky. "So, Miss Sanchez... or can I call you Rosita?"


"Rosita is fine, Mr. Mariano." I say maintaining my professionalism. Once I am able to partner with Eclipse, meeting Braiden would be as easy as taking a lollipop from a baby.


"Then call me Julian," he says. "So, Rosita, I have to ask... what exactly do you have in mind for your investment in Eclipse?"


What's up with all these questions? It's like we're on a merry go round, rotating and rotating but still in the same place. You know, there's an irony here. I should rather be questioning him at the police station right now but look who's doing that right now.


"I'm thinking a significant stake, maybe 20%. I'd want to be involved in decision-making, ensure my investment is being used effectively." I say.


"20%, huh? That's a big chunk. What makes you think you can handle the... complexities of our operation?"


"I've dealt with complex business deals before, Julian. I'm not just a fashion designer– I'm a savvy investor."


He stands and walks to a cupboard and picks up a folder. He opens it and reavels the document inside.


"Alright. Let's discuss the terms of your investment. I think we can work out a deal that benefits both of us. "As you can see, we're offering a 10% return on investment, guaranteed. But I'd be willing to bump that up to 15% if you're willing to commit to a longer-term partnership."


I scan the documents, happy about the pecent he is offering but I try to suppress my excitement. "15% is decent, but I'd want a seat on the board. I need to ensure my investment is being used effectively."


"A seat on the board... that's doable. But I'd want something in return."


"What do you mean?" I ask him.


"Let's finalize the investment first." He says.


"I would like to know what you want in return Mr. Mariano," I say. I am curious about what he wants. What if I can't provide it for him? What if it's risky or has to involve me in something illegal? I know this would happen anyway but I want to know what exactly.


" My lawyer will draw up the contract, and we can finalize the details." He says, totally brushing away my question.


I stand up, take my bag and make my way to the door. I know he would definitely stop me and tell me what he wants. He can't let me slip away from his fingers like that. He stands up and holds my wrist. I take my hand out of his grip and turn to face him.
