Prologue


I sat on the podium, together with my colleague officers as my heart raced like crazy. Today was my graduation ceremony into PNP. Gosh! I can't believe this is actually real. I pinched myself, "Ouch," that hurt, it was real!


My mind flew back to that day, when I was in 5th grade in Elementary school. We were told we would be having a Career Day ceremony. I was probably...eleven then.


That day, I was grinning from ear to ear when I got home. Mom asked me why I was happy and I told her about the upcoming event.


"That's amazing. What does my baby want to be?" She asks and starts tickling me. I throw my legs in the air, laughing hard. God! My laughter could have gotten me a Guiness World Record! She finally stops after my tireless pleas.


"Stop it mommy, I'm not a baby anymore," I say. "I'm eleven and I'm in 5th grade," I say to her.


"Aww... My baby's all grown now. So tell me, what do you want to be on Career Day?" She asks one more time.


"Uhm... I think, I'm gonna be... I'm not gonna tell you until daddy comes," I say and stick my tongue out at her.


"Come on Maria's please tell me. I will buy you a vanilla ice cream tomorrow before school," She says and raises an eyebrow at me.


"Mmm... Vanilla ice cream, wait for daddy," I say, tapping my chin with my index finger weighing the options.


"No, and its final," I say putting my hand on my waist. " I would never surrender to you Mrs. Maria Alcaroio like daddy does. You are under arrest" I say holding my hand out like it was a gun.


Mom held her belly and fell to the floor laughing. Geez, was the laughter thing in our genes?


"What's going on here?" Someone asks and I turn around to see dad. I run to the door and hug him.


"Daddy!" I squeal. He hugs me and carries me to his chest and secures me with his other hand.


"Having a great time without me?" He asks me.


"Daddy, I want to be a police officer for Career Day," I say to him.


"Really?" He asks me.


"Yes daddy, I want to be like you when I grow up," I say to him.


"I'm so proud and happy to have a young police officer in this house," dad says and kisses my forehead. "What has your mom said about it?" He asks.


"I haven't told her yet. I was waiting for you", I say to dad giggling. He laughs and puts me down, looking at mom.


Mom stood there with her arms crossed, and pretended to be angry. "Really, so now you have left me out huh?" Mom asks dad playfully.


"Oops... My bad. How could I do that? You're the queen of my heart," dad says and walks up to her.


" Only your heart?" Mom asks and steps closer to dad.


" And my body," dad says and moves closer closing the gap between them.


I squeeze my eyes shut, my hands covering my face to avoid seeing them kiss. I was disgusted by all their smooches and kisses. I was a child for crying out loud!


Suddenly, a loud crack echoes through the air like a twig snapping underfoot, but a thousand times louder, a sound that shattered something inside me.


I thought mom and dad were about to kiss like they always did. I waited for the smooch sound, waited for them to laugh and tell me to stop being silly. But... there was absolute silence.


My hands slowly dropped, and my eyes fluttered open. No kiss. Mom and Dad were on the floor, collapsed together like puppets cut from strings. Blood was spreading and pooling around them. Before I ran to them, my eyes flashed through the curtains, the silhouette of a man holding a gun that had a diamond emblem of a bird on the grip. An eagle, maybe? Or was it a logo...?


But it was gone. The person was already gone. Then I was on the floor with mom and dad.


"Maria...baby...we love you..." Dad said and his voice sounded as if it was fading.


I felt like I was in slow motion. My legs felt heavy. My eyes couldn't hold what I was seeing, I didn't understand anything.


"Marie...we love you..." Mom said and used her hand to brush my chin.


At that moment, I wished I hadn't closed my eyes. Dad's hand reached out to me, like he was trying to touch me one last time. Then...he stopped.


I turned to look at mom, but her eyes went still and dad didn't move. The sound of the gun kept playing in my head like it was stuck on repeat.


They were gone. I was screaming. Or maybe I wasn't. I don't know. Then, my world went black.


My eyes flutter open and I find myself in a hospital room. The sterile smell of the room choking me.


I see a nurse standing by me and checking the machines. When she sees that I am awake, she rushes out of the room. She comes back in with Uncle Max, my father's best friend, also his colleague in the force. He looks worried and exhausted. He smiles and walks to my bed.


Uncle Max reaches for my hands and hold them. "Marian... sweetheart... I'm so glad you're awake." "I thought... I thought I would lose you too."


Suddenly, the pictures of my parents lying in the pool of blood comes to my mind. They told me they loved me and would never let me be alone but they have left me. A sob catches in my throat, I want to shout and scream, but I can't find my voice. I blink for several hours with tears streaming down my face.


"Where are they?" I manage to ask him.


"That night, an anonymous person phoned the police and reported the incident. We came together with an ambulance to your house. Marian, when we got to the hospital, your parents were already dead, but you were in coma.


We had them buried. You've been in coma for six weeks, the doctor said it was a trauma-induced coma," he says.


Six weeks? I hate myself for not being able to save them that night. It was all my fault, I shouldn't have closed my eyes. Maybe, I could have seen the man earlier and warned them so they wouldn't be hit by the bullet. All this would not have happened then.


"Did the police find anything?" I ask him.


"We... We didn’t find evidence, but I think it was Arnaldo... Arnaldo Mariano. He's the owner of Diamante Corporation. Arnaldo also owns a swanky casino. It's a hub for big players, POGs, and shady deals. He uses the casino to clean money from trafficking, smuggling, etc. The VIP patrons include corrupt officials. Your dad was tasked to investigate about him few weeks before your parents were shot.


I know this is all to much for you to understand. But I want you to know that, I would help you to find justice for your parents. The PNP just left the case as it was because many of them are afraid to go against Arnaldo. We will bring him down together." Uncle Max says and I am just staring at him. I am lost in my own world, with questions which do not have answers.


"Here's the thing, Marian... someone leaked info to Mariano. Someone on the inside. The PNP keeps officers anonymous, cover tracks with fake identities... but they found your house and that is why your parents were killed." Uncle Max continues.


Acold dread spreads in me. How can someone be so cruel? I thought every police officer wants law and order, so how could they do this to my father? What was their reason for doing that?


"It's a miracle you're alive Marian. They would have killed you too, Marian. Because you were there, and you could have seen something. They wanted you silenced."


"Why didn't they shoot me too. I want to die, I can't leave without them. Mom! Dad!" I scream, behaving in a way that I couldn't understand, tears flowing down my cheeks. I wanted to get out of that room, jump off a hill, or a tall building and die. I try to remove the tubes snaked into me. They were strangling me. HowamIgoingtolive my life without my parents by my side?


Uncle Max is saying something and is trying to stop me, but I can't hear a word. It's like I'm a wild animal on chase. A doctor and a nurse rush in. The nurse pins me on the bed and the the doctor injects something on my hand.


"Mom...Dad," I say weakly and then, everything goes black.


"Officer Mariana Alcaroio!" the emcee called out.


I tried to keep it together. I wish mom and dad were here. They always encouraged me to work hard to chase my dreams. They were a part of my dreams, but here I am, living it without them. How was I supposed to do that?


I walked up to my uncle Max, Police Major General. He was standing tall in his uniform. I called out, "Sir!"


He smiled and said to me, "Officer Alcaroio, it is my honor to congratulate you on this remarkable achievement. As you receive this badge, remember you're a symbol of hope, justice, and protection."


He paused, and then continued, "Marian, from the moment you walked into the Police Academy, I knew you had fire in your veins. Growing up, you watched your father, late Police Lieutenant Carlo Alcaroio, put on this uniform. You would say, 'Daddy, one day, I want to be like you'. Today, this dream is real.


You started with the basics, sweating it out in training and pushed the limits. Then you took the leap and went to PNP Officer Candidate School and you crushed it. Now, as a Commissioned Officer, you're not just enforcing law... you're leading, inspiring, and protecting our communities with honor.


Marian, I couldn't be prouder. Your parents would have been proud too. Welcome to the ranks of leaders! The PNP needs your passion.


Congratulations, Mariana Alcaroio!"
