Chapter Fourteen: City Lights and Surprise Heartbeats


One month.


That’s how long it had been since she’d left the farm—since she ran barefoot into his arms and kissed him like she was writing a love letter with her mouth.


Now, the city hummed around her again. The traffic. The cafes. The skyline she used to call home. It all looked the same. But she wasn’t.


---


Alara sat on the floor of her bedroom, back against her bed, phone pressed to her cheek, giggling softly as River grumbled on the other end.


“So the chicken actually pecked you?”


“It ambushed me.”


“River. It’s two pounds of feathers and attitude.”


“It has intent.”


She laughed, curling her legs under her. Her long black curls were tied up with a pencil, oversized shirt slipping off one shoulder, blue eyes bright.


They talked every night.


Texted constantly.


Called even when they had nothing to say.


But still… She missed him.


Even if she wouldn’t admit it out loud.


“I wish you were here,” she mumbled, voice softer now. “I mean, not like permanently. Or maybe… maybe—”


“You don’t have to explain,” he said, cutting her off gently.


Silence stretched for a beat.


Then—


“Hey... speaking of that…”


Her phone buzzed.


Incoming call: River (Farm Boy 💛)


She stared.


He was already on the line. Why was he calling?


Confused, she swiped to answer. “Hello?” she said with curiousity.


“Open your door.”


Her heart stopped. “What?”


“Alara. Open...your... door.”


She scrambled up so fast she knocked over her iced coffee and kicked a shoe across the room.


Her best friend, Zara—sitting on the bed painting her nails—raised a brow. “What’s going on? Why do you look like you saw a ghost? Or a tax collector?”


Alara didn’t answer. She ran. Flung open the front door—


And there he was. River.


Tall, sun-kissed, messy-haired, leaning against the hallway wall like he belonged in a romance drama. Tight black tee. Faded jeans. Boots. That signature half-scowl, half-smirk expression he wore like armor. Her breath caught.


“Hey,” he said, scratching the back of his neck.


She stared, “You’re… here.”


“Been saying that for a minute.”


“How?”


“I told you I hated the city.”


“You drove here to complain in person?”


He grinned. “No. I came because I missed you.”


And just like that—


She crashed into him. No hesitation. No plan. Just bare feet, fast heartbeats, and arms around his neck. He caught her. Hugged her so tight she couldn’t breathe. And she didn’t want to.


Zara peeked down the hall, eyes wide. “Oh. My. GOD,” she whispered. “It’s Farm Thor.”


River looked over Alara’s shoulder and raised a brow. “Who’s that?”


“Zara,” Alara mumbled. “She’s been calling you ‘Farm Thor’ since I got back.”


River smirked. “I’m honored. I think.”


Alara laughed into his neck. “You smell like hay and bad decisions.”


“You smell like lip gloss and trouble.”


She pulled back just enough to see his face. “How long are you staying?”


He cupped her cheek. “As long as you want.”


She bit her lip, eyes full of something fragile. “I want forever.”


His smile softened, “Then I guess I’m home.”


Both of them smiled. The kind of smile that said everything they needed to say without words.
