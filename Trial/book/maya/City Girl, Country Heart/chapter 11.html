Chapter Eleven: Moonlight, Confessions, and a Goat Named Regret


The stars stretched wide above them, scattered across the sky like glitter tossed by a careless god. Alara walked beside River along the edge of the pasture, arms folded under her chest, denim shorts dusted from the day’s work, her long black hair catching the breeze. River had his hands in his pockets, shoulders tense, eyes darting sideways every few seconds like he couldn’t stop checking she was still there. They’d been quiet for five minutes. Not awkward quiet. Not angry quiet. Just… charged. Like a moment building itself carefully—one breath at a time.


Finally, she exhaled. “So… about earlier.”


He tensed.


She glanced at him. “I mean, the kiss. The toe. My face. Your chest. That whole... situation.”


He nodded slowly. “Yeah.”


“I panicked.”


“I noticed.”


“I swore I wasn’t gonna fall into your arms again.”


“And yet here we are. What’s that, time number five?”


“Six, if you count the horse stall thing.”


“Definitely counts.”


She laughed softly, toeing a pebble. “You’re not mad, are you?”


He stopped walking. “Why would I be mad?”


She turned to face him, arms still crossed—defensive, nervous.


“I don’t know. Because I’m loud. And messy. And I yell a lot. And faint. And insult your truck. And—”


“Alara.”


His voice was steady, firm.


She blinked. “Yeah?”


“I like that about you.”


She froze. “Wait, what?”


He took a step closer, eyes darker under the stars. “You drive me crazy. You make everything louder, harder, messier. And I like it.”


She blinked. “Oh.”


He raised a brow. “You’re not gonna say anything?”


She chewed her lip. “I kinda like you too. But like—violently. Like, I want to kiss you and punch you in the same breath.”


He laughed.


She sighed dramatically. “This is terrible. I hate emotions. They make me feel things.”


“You’re supposed to feel things.”


“I was doing fine before you came into my life with your muscles and your ‘fix-it’ energy.”


“And I was fine before a storm in eyeliner fell into my truck.”


They were quiet again. Standing too close. Heartbeats caught mid-thump.


Then River tilted his head. Her breath hitched. His hand slid gently to her waist. She leaned in. So did he. Their lips hovered—barely a breath apart—


“MAAAAAA,” Alara screamed.


A goat. A goat had trotted up behind her and nuzzled her leg like it was asking for hay and ruining lives.


“IS THAT A GOAT?!” she shrieked.


River burst into full, genuine, chest-shaking laughter.


“Yep. That’s Regret.”


“Regret?!”


“He gets in the way of a lot of things.”


Alara was still frozen, goat sniffing her thigh. “WHY IS IT LOOKING AT ME LIKE THAT?”


“He probably ships us.”


“I swear to God I will suplex this goat.”


River was still laughing as he stepped forward, gently nudged the goat away, and turned back to her.


She opened her mouth to say something snarky—


But he beat her to it. He kissed her. No stumbling. No accidents. Just him—warm, firm, sure—pressing his lips to hers under the stars, gently, but with the kind of slow-burn intensity that made her knees forget how to function. She melted into him. Kissed him back. Fingers curling in his shirt. Her heart screaming louder than the goat.


When they finally pulled apart, she was breathless. “You kissed me,” she whispered, dazed.


“You didn’t scream,” he replied, smirking.


She punched his shoulder. “I’m still suing someone.”


Regret bleated from the side, totally unbothered.


River smiled. “Next time I’ll lock up the livestock.”


Alara grinned.


“I kinda like you too, Farm Boy.”
