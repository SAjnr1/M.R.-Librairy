Chapter Ten: Biscuits, Bro Talk, and Blurted Confessions


Grace was elbow-deep in biscuit dough, humming along to an old blues tune on the radio when Alara came bursting in like she’d just fought a mountain lion.


“Grace. Grace. GRACE,” Alara yelled.


“Sweetheart,” Grace said calmly, without looking up, “are you bleeding, pregnant, or on fire?”


Alara slapped her palms against the counter. “I ACCIDENTALLY KISSED RIVER!”


Grace’s hands stilled. She looked up, blinking.


“You what now?”


“It wasn’t planned! It was gravity! Physics! Betrayal!”


Grace blinked again. “Where on your list of enemies does gravity fall now?”


“Just under snakes. JUST UNDER.”


Grace wiped her hands on a towel and leaned on the counter. “Okay. Start over.”


Alara paced.


“I stubbed my toe on the porch step, because this house has a vendetta against me. He was coming in, I was going out, and my face just—collided....with his face....our lips brushed. And then…”


“And then?” Grace pressed, eyes twinkling.


Alara froze. “...And then I might have liked it.”


Grace’s lips twitched. “Oh.”


“No. No ‘oh.’ This is a crisis. He’s rude. And infuriating. And sweaty. But also strong. And hot. And—WHY DID HE HAVE TO SMELL SO GOOD?!”


Grace laughed. “You kissed the boy and realized he’s a man.”


Alara covered her face. “I kissed the haystack.”


“You kissed the farm.”


“I liked it.”


Grace patted her shoulder gently. “Welcome to denial, baby.”


---


Outside, River stood near the edge of the field, kicking at the dirt like it owed him money.


Josh strolled up behind him, sipping from a soda can, already smirking.


“Gonna tell me why you look like someone slapped your soul outta your body?”


River grunted.


Josh took that as encouragement.


“Something happened. You’re twitchier than usual. Is it Alara? Did she call you a ‘brooding man-ham’ again?”


“No,” River muttered.


Josh raised an eyebrow. “Then what? She punch you? Call your truck ugly again?”


River exhaled sharply and said through gritted teeth, “We kissed.”


Josh blinked.


And then grinned so wide it should’ve been illegal. “You WHAT?”


“It was an accident.”


“Uh-huh.”


“She tripped. Again.”


Josh sipped dramatically. “And somehow, her lips just fell on yours?”


“Yes!”


“And you just stood there, all ‘oops, this is happening’? No thoughts? Just cowboy mode?”


River ran a hand through his hair. “It wasn’t like that.”


Josh tilted his head. “Did you hate it?”


River opened his mouth.


Closed it.


And then muttered, “...I didn’t hate it.”


Josh’s jaw dropped.


“Oh my God. Oh my God. You like her.”


“No I don’t.”


“You LIKE her.”


“I kind of like her.”


Josh let out a whoop, nearly tripping over a rock. “This is the best thing that’s ever happened. You, the emotionally constipated scarecrow, caught feelings for the hot storm in yoga shorts.”


“I hate you,” River said.


“No you don’t. You hate admitting stuff. But this?” He clapped his friend on the back. “This is gonna be fun.”


---


Dinner was unusually quiet.


Alara sat picking at her food like it might betray her.


River kept his eyes glued to his plate, jaw tight, hand clenched around his fork like it owed him money.


Every time they made brief eye contact, it was like someone lit a firework under the table.


Josh, Cal, and Mitch exchanged looks like toddlers at a wedding who knew something was about to explode.


Grace sipped her sweet tea, completely unbothered.


Until finally—


River stood up, muttered, “I’m going out.”


Alara stood up at the same time. “Me too.”


Their eyes met. Held. Josh nearly choked on a biscuit.


Grace leaned back in her chair with a smile. “And so it begins.”
