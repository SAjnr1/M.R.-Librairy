Chapter Eighteen: Retaliation


It started with silence. Not the quiet kind. The unnatural kind. Saint noticed it first.


Birds — gone.


Squirrels — still.


No wind. No forest breath.


He looked up from where he was oiling his gun and met Euphoria’s eyes across the room. She felt it too. A stillness in the air that tasted like metal.


Then — crack.


The explosion blew the side of the cabin apart. Euphoria hit the floor, pain bursting in her shoulder as wood and heat and noise shattered the world around her. She heard Saint yelling — or maybe it was just her pulse screaming in her ears. He was over her in seconds, checking her for bleeding. She coughed hard.


“I’m fine,” she rasped. “Just — go.”


“They’re not after me this time.”


Her eyes locked onto his. And then she understood. The attack wasn’t an assassination. It was a message.


And the message was: we can get to her too.


Saint hauled her up, wrapped her arm around his shoulder, and pulled her into the secret crawl tunnel beneath the floorboards — a backup escape hatch he hoped they’d never use. They emerged in the trees, breathing in smoke and earth. Shots rang out behind them. Saint threw her behind a tree, returned fire — three, four shots, tight and clean. But it wasn’t enough. There were too many.


He reached into his boot, pulled a small black flash drive, and shoved it into her hand. He said,“If I don’t make it—”


“Don’t you dare,” she interfered


“—you take this to a man named Marcus Vale in D.C. He’s the only one who can erase everything the Families built.”


She grabbed his face. “You’re not dying. I haven’t fallen for a man just to bury him.”


They kissed — not soft. Not sweet. A kiss like a promise sealed in blood. Then Euphoria did something Saint didn’t expect. She grabbed his pistol and ran toward the gunfire.


“Euphoria!”


But she was already moving, weaving through the trees like a ghost with vengeance in her veins. Because now it was personal. They tried to scare her. But they forgot one thing. She wasn't a soldier. She was a surgeon. And she knew exactly where to cut next.
