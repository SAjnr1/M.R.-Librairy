Chapter Twenty: The Surgeon’s Game


Saint was already sketching out attack routes, safe exits, surveillance dead zones. Every movement crisp, efficient, lethal.


But Euphoria wasn’t watching the blueprints. She was watching him. Something inside her had shifted. She wasn’t just reacting anymore — she was calculating.


“We don’t hit them,” she said quietly.


Saint paused mid-sentence. “What?”


“We don’t play their game. We play mine.”


He raised an eyebrow. “What game is that?”


Euphoria walked to the table, cleared the plans off it with a sweep of her arm, and pulled out her phone.


“I’m a surgeon, Saint. I don’t just cut. I study. I prepare. And I know exactly where to slice to make a giant bleed slow and deep.”


She tapped her phone, opened her encrypted hospital database app — something only a senior trauma physician could access. She pulled up a name. Not a medical record. Not a patient. An alias.


Attached to it were lab tests, bloodwork, a DNA match. She turned the screen toward Saint. “Your old handler, Nicoli Fenech? He came in under a fake name three months ago. I ran tests. He has a clotting disorder. One bad accident and he’ll bleed out before help arrives.”


Saint stared. “You knew who he was?”


“I suspected. I confirm things before I slice.”


He blinked once. Slowly. “Jesus Christ.”


She kept going. “He’s not untouchable. No one is. The Families? They’re not made of ghosts. They’re made of men — and every man has a pressure point. A kid, a mistress, a medical dependency, a mistake they think no one knows about.”


“And you know where to cut.”


“I know how to cut.”


Saint stepped closer, eyes unreadable. “You want to destroy them with… records?”


“I want to cripple them with information. Threaten their illusion of power. Make them sweat. Make them panic. Once they’re scared, they’re sloppy.”


“You’re talking psychological warfare.”


She looked up. “No. I’m talking surgical precision.”


He stared at her like she was someone brand new. Maybe she was.


Then slowly, he smiled. “You’re terrifying.”


“Welcome to my world.”
