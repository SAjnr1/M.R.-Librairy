Chapter Twenty-Two: Aftermath


The forest was still again. Not the hunted kind of still — the kind that came after. After gunfire. After vengeance. After death. Nicoli Fenech’s body lay in the dirt, cooling beneath the rising sun.


Euphoria stood beside Saint, her hand in his, both of them streaked with smoke and blood. Her shoulder ached, and her lungs were raw from running, from shouting, from fighting. But she was still standing.


Alive


Free


Saint didn’t say a word at first. Neither did she. They just stared into the trees, side by side, the air around them thick with things they hadn’t said — things they hadn’t dared to say yet.


Finally, Saint turned to her. “You didn’t just save me,” he said quietly. “You changed the war.”


Euphoria looked at him, pale grey eyes soft now. “I didn’t want a war. I just wanted to save a man bleeding in the street.”


His voice cracked. “And now?”


“Now?” She exhaled. “I want to save myself. Us. Whatever the hell this is. But I need to know…” She trailed off, unsure of her own question.


Saint stepped closer, hands cupping her face. “I've killed for less. But I’ve never lived for anyone… until you.”


Tears welled, unbidden. Not from pain. From release. “You terrify me,” she whispered.


“You undo me,” he answered.


She leaned into him, forehead against his. And in that moment — broken, bruised, bloodstained — they found the calm they’d both been running from.


He didn’t ask if she’d stay.


She didn’t ask if he’d go.


Because finally, they knew.


They weren’t just falling anymore.


They’d already fallen. And neither of them wanted to get back up.
