Chapter Sixteen: Safehouse Confessions


The safehouse was in the woods.A two-hour drive out of the city, hidden off a forgotten highway, tucked behind pine trees and silence. It looked like a cabin, but everything inside said otherwise — steel-reinforced windows, bulletproof doors, emergency lights, and three separate escape routes.Saint hadn’t said a word since they left the city. Euphoria hadn’t asked. Not yet.


But now, as the door locked behind them and the forest swallowed them whole, the weight of it all crashed down. She stood in the middle of the small living room, arms folded, gun still tucked in her waistband like she’d done it for years.


“I need answers,” she said.


Saint leaned against the door, face unreadable. “I figured.”


“Who were they?”


“Kill team. Eastern bloc. Ex-military. They freelance for the Families sometimes when someone breaks the code.”


She narrowed her eyes. “You broke the code?”


“I disappeared. Left a job unfinished. That’s enough.”


“Why?”


Silence.


“Why did you run?” she pressed. “Why did you come into my life? My world? I patched you up. I trusted you. I kissed you. And now I’m running for my life because of something you won't even explain.”


Saint exhaled slowly.


“I was supposed to kill someone.”


“Who?”


“A woman,” he said quietly. “A witness. Not a threat. Just a loose end.”


He looked up, and something in his eyes was hollowed out.


“She reminded me of you.”


Euphoria froze.


“That night, on the street,” he continued. “I walked away. Dropped the gun. Took a bullet from my own partner in the ribs for it. The Families don’t tolerate weakness. I bled all the way to your block.”


“And you just happened to stumble onto a trauma surgeon’s doorstep?”


“No. I followed you.”


Euphoria’s heart cracked in her chest. “You knew me?”,she asked


“Not well. Just enough to know you saved people. That you’d help if you saw someone dying.”


Her voice dropped to a whisper. “So it was a setup?”


“No,” Saint said instantly, stepping toward her. “It was survival. But after that night… after you touched me like I was human… it stopped being about survival.”


He stopped in front of her, voice raw now. “You made me want to be a man again. Not a ghost. Not a weapon. Just… yours.”


Tears stung the back of her eyes, but she blinked them away. “I don’t know how to be with someone like you,” she whispered.


“Then let me show you how I’d fight for you,” he said to assure her.


“You already are,” she replied.


They stood in silence, too many words between them and none big enough to fix it.


Then her voice broke through. “If they come again… we finish this. Together.”


Saint looked at her — really looked — and nodded once. “Together,” Saint assured her.
