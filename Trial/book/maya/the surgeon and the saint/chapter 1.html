Chapter One: Blood on the Pavement


The city hummed under a velvet sky, streetlights casting pale halos on rain-slicked sidewalks. Dr. Euphoria Vale tugged her coat tighter around her, the chill of midnight curling around her collarbones. Her shift at St. Augustus Memorial had ended two hours late, her last patient a boy with a mangled leg and a mother who cried like the world was ending.


Euphoria walked home, heels clicking in steady rhythm, her thoughts drifting between fatigue and her empty apartment. She didn't mind solitude. It was predictable. Safe.


Until she heard it—a groan. Low. Animalistic. Pained.


She stopped.


At the mouth of an alley, a man lay slumped against the bricks, a smear of red painting the wall beside him. One hand clutched his side, blood leaking between his fingers. His eyes locked onto hers: sharp, wary, still fighting.


“Help me,” he rasped.


Euphoria’s breath caught. She should call 911. Let someone else handle it. But something in his gaze—desperation, vulnerability, a strange pull—froze her.


She crouched beside him, instincts taking over. “You’ve been shot. I’m a surgeon.”


He exhaled a curse. “No hospitals.”


“Then you’re going to die.”


He chuckled, grimacing. “Not tonight, angel.


That was the beginning.
