Chapter Seventeen: Leverage


The silence in the safehouse had settled like dust. They were no longer running — for now — but that didn’t mean they were safe. Euphoria sat curled on the old leather couch, legs tucked beneath her, flipping through a worn medical journal she’d found in her bag. The words barely registered. Her mind wouldn’t let her focus.


Not after what Saint had confessed. Not after what they’d become. He was out back, sharpening a blade on a flat river stone, arms bare, his focus deadly. It wasn’t just muscle memory. It was meditation. Preparing. For what, she didn’t know. Until the phone rang.


A burner, one of several he kept in a black box locked in the kitchen cabinet. Saint was already at her side by the second ring, phone in hand, answering without a word. A long pause.


Then a low, guttural voice came through on speaker. “She’s pretty, doc. Looks strong, too. Wonder how much blood she’s got to spare.” Euphoria sat up. Saint froze.


The voice laughed. “Check your inbox.” The call ended.


Saint moved fast, fingers flying across his backup phone. A single photo downloaded. It was grainy. Shot through a windshield. But Euphoria recognized the woman instantly. Dr. Kenna Mays. Her closest friend at the hospital. Her former roommate. Her sister in everything but blood. She was standing outside the trauma wing, hands full of files, eyes scanning the parking lot like she sensed something wrong.


Euphoria’s stomach turned to ice. “They know who she is,” she whispered.


Saint’s voice was flat. “They’re saying if I don’t come back and finish the job I walked away from, they’ll hurt her. Bad.”


Euphoria stood, grabbing her bag. “Then we go to her.”


“No.” Saint grabbed her wrist, firm but gentle. “That’s exactly what they want. Use her to draw me out. Lure you back into the city.”


“And what do we do? Wait until they kill someone else I love?”


Saint’s jaw clenched, war warring in his eyes. “You shouldn’t have to choose this.”


“I already have.”


She stepped into him, close enough for him to feel her heartbeat against his chest.


“You’re not in this alone anymore. You never were.”


He looked down at her. “You sure?”


“I’m sure.”


He pulled her into a rough, desperate kiss — not passion, not sweetness. Claim, promise.


When they pulled apart, his voice was steel. “Then we hit them first.”
