Chapter Three: Shadows and Secrets


He woke up screaming.


Euphoria leapt from the kitchen with a scalpel still in hand. He thrashed on the couch, breath ragged, eyes wild—then focused on her, recognizing the woman who saved him.


“You stitched me up,” he said, voice hoarse.


She nodded. “Didn’t think you’d thank me. What were you dreaming about?”


His silence said everything.


“I won’t ask questions,” she added, sitting across from him. “But if danger’s going to follow you into my apartment, I need to know how to protect myself.”


He studied her. “You’re not scared of me?”


“No,” she said, voice calm and direct. “But I know a killer when I see one.”


A flicker of something passed through his eyes—guilt? Regret?


“I’m not staying long,” he said. “Just until I can move. Then I disappear.”


She didn’t respond right away. Then: “Fine. But until then, you do what I say. This is my space, and I don’t get intimidated easily.”


For the first time, he gave her a genuine smile. “What’s your name?”


“Euphoria.”


He blinked. “That’s... not a name people forget.”


“Neither is yours,” she said, nodding to the pistol on the table beside him. “You going to tell me what it is?”


He hesitated. Then: “Call me Saint.”


That was the beginning of the game—two strangers, circling truth with loaded silences. She didn’t trust him, but she was drawn to him. And he, for the first time in years, wasn’t thinking about his next target.


He was thinking about her.
