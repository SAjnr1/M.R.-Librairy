Chapter Twenty-One: Precision Meets Brutality


Blood blurred the edges of Euphoria’s vision. Her shoulder throbbed. Smoke clung to her skin. Her breath came in ragged gasps as she crouched behind a pine trunk, listening for the men hunting her.


Six... maybe seven. She counted the crunch of boots. The way trained killers tried too hard to be silent.


But she had been in ORs under blackout conditions, saving lives with her bare hands. Pressure didn’t make her crumble — it made her sharper. She wasn’t afraid. She was angry. And anger had precision.


Euphoria gripped the pistol Saint had trained her on. No tremble. No hesitation. Her pulse synced with every decision. Every move. She slipped through the trees like a shadow, circling behind them — then struck. One clean shot. Then another. Two men dropped.


The others scattered, yelling. She didn’t run. She advanced. Another gunman turned, aimed—too slow. She shot him point-blank, muzzle flash lighting the rage in her pale grey eyes.


Then: footsteps. Louder, heavier, Saint. He emerged from the smoke, blood on his temple, fire in his hands. When he saw her — bloodied, standing over bodies — his breath caught. “Damn.”


“You said I’d need to learn to shoot,” she panted.


“I didn’t think you’d be better than me by week three.”


Then — behind him. A shape. Tall. Built like a tank. And wearing a smile that had sent a hundred men to their graves.


Nicoli Fenech.


Euphoria’s gut clenched. Saint turned slowly, every muscle in his body coiled tight. Nicoli stepped forward, casually aiming a silver pistol at them both. “You’ve grown soft,” he said to Saint. “Letting her kill for you. Letting her love you.” He looked at Euphoria. “And you… were supposed to be leverage. Not a goddamn problem.”


Euphoria didn’t flinch. She stepped forward. “Funny. You were supposed to be untouchable. Not bleeding internally from a genetic clotting disorder.”


Nicoli’s face twitched.


Saint blinked.


She smiled. “I treated you once, Nicoli. You came in under an alias. Thought I wouldn’t notice the bruising pattern. The off-the-charts coag results. You’re a walking aneurysm waiting to burst.”


Nicoli raised the gun — furious now. But he didn’t shoot, because Saint was already moving. In a blur, he tackled Nicoli, driving them both into the dirt. Fists flew. Bones cracked. Euphoria rushed to them, but she didn’t need to fire. Saint didn’t stop until Nicoli stopped breathing.


When it was over, Saint stood slowly, chest heaving. Blood on his hands. Jaw clenched.


Euphoria walked up beside him and looked down at the man who’d hunted them for months. Then she dropped Nicoli’s own gun beside his body. “Scalpel,” she whispered. “Meet hammer.”
