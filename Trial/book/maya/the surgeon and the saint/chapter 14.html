Chapter Fourteen: Shadows Return


It started with the envelope. Plain, unmarked, slipped under Euphoria’s door while they slept.


She found it that morning. No postage. No name. Just heavy, ivory paper with one word written in blocky red ink:


RUN.


She stared at it for a long second before calling for Saint.


He was beside her in less than five heartbeats, the mug still in his hand. When he saw the envelope, he didn’t speak, didn’t move, just stared.


“What is it?” she asked, her voice sharper than fear.


Saint took it from her hand, flipped it over, checked the corners, the seal, the weight.


“This is a warning,” he muttered.


“No shit.”


“No,” he said more urgently. “This is their kind of warning.”


Euphoria crossed her arms. “Who’s they?”


He looked up at her then—really looked. “The people I used to work for.”


And there it was. The thing he hadn’t said. Not just assassin. Not just mafia. But owned. By something bigger than him.


“How did they find you?” she asked.


Saint’s jaw tightened. “They didn’t. They found you.” The silence between them turned to ice.


“You said you were done,” she said quietly.


“I was.”


“So what now?”


Saint was already moving—pulling a duffel bag from beneath the couch, stuffing it with cash, weapons, burner phones. He looked up, breath tight. “Now we go. You pack light. I’ll handle the rest.”


Euphoria didn’t move. “You want me to run? From my life? My job? My home?”


“I want you alive.”


“I saved you. I brought you into my world. You don’t get to blow it up and drag me into yours.”


“You think I don’t know that?” he snapped, sharper than he meant to. “You think I haven’t been trying every day not to get you killed?”


The silence that followed was heavier than any threat.


Then softly, painfully:


“I didn’t mean to fall in love with you.” The words slipped out like a secret he hadn’t meant to say.


Euphoria blinked. “Say that again.”


But he didn’t. He couldn’t, because just then, the window shattered. A bullet slammed into the kitchen tile inches from where she’d been standing.


Saint dove forward, wrapped her in his arms, and pulled her down behind the island. Gun drawn, heart racing, eyes sharp, and just like that—


The assassin was back, and this time, the woman he loved was in the line
