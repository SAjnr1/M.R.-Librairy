Epilogue: Grey Eyes and Tiny Heartbeats


The wind rustled the Vermont trees, golden with early autumn. Inside the cabin — their home — laughter curled around old wood and warm sunlight. It was quiet here. Safe. The kind of quiet that didn’t follow trauma... but grew from healing.


Saint stood in the doorway of the kitchen, shirt rolled at the sleeves, tea steeping beside him. He stared at her like he always did. Like she was a miracle he still couldn’t believe he deserved. Euphoria sat on the sunlit couch, one hand on her belly, the other resting on a worn medical journal she’d marked up just that morning. Her white locs were braided back, skin glowing like it always did, but now… there was a softness to her. A fullness.


She was eight months pregnant. And radiant. “You’re staring again,” she said without looking up, a teasing smile tugging at her lips.


Saint walked toward her, tea in hand, kneeling to press his lips against the curve of her stomach. “She’s kicking,” he murmured.


Euphoria nodded. “She’s strong. Just like her father.”


“Stubborn,” he said with a grin. “Like her mother.”


They laughed. The kind of laugh that only came from surviving the impossible and still managing to love anyway.


His wedding band caught the light as he ran his hand across her belly. “Have you decided on a name yet?”


Euphoria raised an eyebrow. “We did that three months ago, Saint.”


“I just like hearing you say it.”


She smiled, “Amara. Amara Thorne-Leone.”


“Grace,” he said softly. “After all we’ve done… she’ll carry none of it.”


“No,” Euphoria whispered, voice thick with emotion. “Only the strength of it.”


He looked up at her then, eyes glassy. “You saved me,” he said.


Euphoria cupped his face, drawing him up to kiss her — slow, deep, forever.


“No,” she whispered. “We saved each other.”


Outside, the world kept spinning. Inside, their world had stopped. And started over. This time, with a heartbeat softer than gunfire.


And a future brighter than vengeance.


The End
