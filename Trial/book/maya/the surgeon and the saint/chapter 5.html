Chapter Five: The Rules


Euphoria stood at the foot of the couch, arms crossed under her chest. The man—Saint, as he called himself—was upright now, a little steadier, a little too comfortable.


“We need to set boundaries,” she said.


Saint raised an eyebrow, reclining with that smug ease she was starting to find irritatingly attractive. “You mean more than you stitching up a gunshot wound and letting me bleed all over your designer carpet?”


She didn’t flinch. “Rule one: No weapons on the table. I don’t care what your job is. You keep your piece in your pants or out of my sight.”


A smirk tugged at his lips. “Noted.”


“Rule two: No sneaking around at night. You wake me, I toss you out.”


His smirk vanished. “Fair.”


“Rule three—”


“You always this fun?”


Euphoria narrowed her pale grey eyes. “Rule three: No names. Real ones. I’m not interested in whatever ghost you were before you landed on my street. You leave that behind at the door.”


Something flickered behind his eyes—pain, maybe. Or maybe just memory.


“You got it, Doc.”


She paused. That nickname. He said it with too much heat. Too much edge.


“You’re a guest in my space. Don’t make me regret this.”


Saint sat up straighter, his voice soft but serious. “You already do.”


Silence stretched between them. She hated how true that felt. Because she didn’t regret saving him—not yet. But she didn’t know what she’d invited in either.


He reached for the glass of water she’d set out and took a sip, then looked at her like he could see through all the layers she wore to keep people out.


“I don’t know what kind of woman brings home a bleeding stranger,” he said. “But I think I want to find out.”


Euphoria turned away, heat rising up her neck. She didn’t answer.
