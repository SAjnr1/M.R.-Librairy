Chapter Two: The Stranger in Her Bed


Euphoria dragged the stranger—barely conscious—through the back door of her apartment and laid him on her living room floor. Blood had soaked through his side again, despite the pressure she'd applied in the alley. She didn’t have time to panic.


She worked by instinct—cutting off his shirt, disinfecting the wound, stitching him in tight, clean loops. His muscles tensed under her touch, jaw clenched from the pain, but he didn’t make a sound. Not even when the needle bit deep.


By the time she was done, sweat beaded her forehead and her hands were shaking. She looked at him—beautiful in a dark, dangerous way. Not just handsome. Engineered. His body was a weapon. The gun in his waistband confirmed that.


Still unconscious, he looked more human than assassin. Vulnerable. She covered him with a blanket, not knowing why she was keeping him here.


Maybe it was the way he’d called her “angel.” Maybe it was the way his blood hadn’t scared her.


Maybe, deep down, she was tired of being alone.
