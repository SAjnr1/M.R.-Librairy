Chapter Thirteen: Morning After


Euphoria woke before sunrise. The storm had passed. The city outside her window was washed clean, glittering in the early grey light. Her room was still, the silence thick with something new. She rolled over.


Saint was asleep beside her. He didn’t look like the man she’d found bleeding on the pavement weeks ago. He didn’t even look like the sharp-eyed, brooding ghost who’d haunted her living room for days. Now, in the soft light, his features were unguarded. Younger. Almost peaceful.


His chest rose and fell with a quiet rhythm, one arm resting between them, fingers curled like he’d reached for her in sleep but hadn’t quite dared. Euphoria studied him for a long moment.What had they done?


Not just the kiss. Not even the night spent together, tangled in each other’s heat and silence. No, it was the trust. The letting in. That was what scared her most. She sat up quietly and slipped out of bed, padding to the kitchen in nothing but her robe. The coffee machine was loud in the stillness. She liked it. Something about the clatter grounded her.


“Didn’t think you’d still be here,” a voice said behind her. Saint stood in the doorway, barefoot, wearing nothing but his sweatpants and a look she couldn’t quite read.


She met his eyes. “Why wouldn’t I be?”


He shrugged, stepping into the room. “People disappear after things get real.”


“You do that?” she asked.


“Used to.”


He moved toward her, slow but sure, and stopped just close enough that she could feel his warmth. “I haven’t touched anyone like that in a long time,” he said.


She raised an eyebrow. “You mean emotionally or physically?”


“Both.”


She handed him a mug. “Same.”


Saint took it from her, but didn’t look away. “So what are we doing?”


“I don’t know yet,” she admitted. “But I’m not sorry.”


“Me neither.”


They stood like that for a beat—quiet, holding mugs, standing in a kitchen that suddenly felt more like a home.


Then she said it:


“If you break my heart, I won’t cry. I’ll bury you in a hospital basement where no one will ever find you.”


Saint chuckled. “That’s fair.”


They smiled at each other. Not wide. Not open, but real.


And for the first time, Saint didn’t feel like a ghost. And Euphoria didn’t feel alone.
