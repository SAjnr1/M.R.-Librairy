Chapter Nineteen: Surgical Strike


The emails went out at midnight.


Encrypted. Timed.


One to a corrupt city councilman with ties to the Solari syndicate — attaching hospital scans that proved Nicoli Fenech’s clotting disorder, along with falsified surgery requests listed under stolen aliases.


Another to an Internal Affairs officer Euphoria knew through her time treating gunshot victims — this one carried proof that the Families had doctors on their payroll. That the clean blood tests on a certain high-profile underboss were forged.


The third?


That went directly to a rival cartel.


One document. One name. One weakness.


Saint stood behind her as she clicked “Send.”


“You’re burning a lot of bridges,” he said quietly.


Euphoria didn’t look up. “Good. I brought the matches.”


Then she opened her final file — a collection of personal contacts she'd treated over the years. Some were dirty cops. Others were just desperate. But she knew every single one of them had a price. And she had leverage.


“Help me,” her text read, “and I make you disappear from every system. Hurt me… and I hand your blood test to every agency that hates you.”


She hit send and leaned back, chest rising and falling slowly.


“Now,” she said, “we wait.”


But they didn’t have to wait long. By morning, the Families were in chaos. Three lieutenants pulled back their soldiers. Two safehouses went dark.


And Nicoli Fenech? He called Saint directly. No threats. No taunts. Just two words: “She’s dangerous.”


Saint smiled into the phone. “Yeah. Isn’t she beautiful?”


The line went dead.


He turned to Euphoria. “They’re retreating. Not because of me. Because of you.”


She looked up at him, eyes like a storm.


“No,” she said. “They’re regrouping. And the next move? That’s ours.”
