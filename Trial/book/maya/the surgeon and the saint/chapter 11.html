Chapter Eleven: The Shift


She didn’t call the ex back. She thought about it. Even drafted a reply. But she couldn’t send it. She thought about it. Even drafted a reply. But she couldn’t send it. Not with Saint’s voice echoing in her head. "I feel it. And I don't like it."


He hadn’t spoken to her in two days. Still moved around the apartment like a ghost—but colder this time. No more folded towels. No coffee in the morning. He slept on the couch with his back to the room, and the distance between them felt like a wall built brick by brick out of every word they’d never said. By Thursday night, she’d had enough. She walked out of her room in a robe, barefoot, and stood at the edge of the living room. Saint looked up from the floor. He was cleaning his sidearm — slowly, methodically, like a ritual. His shirt was off, and the soft lamplight caught every scar on his torso. Some jagged, some precise. Survivor’s marks. He didn’t speak. Just waited.


“You’re really good at disappearing without leaving,” she said softly.


He didn’t look at her. “Didn’t know I owed you conversation.”


Euphoria stepped closer. “You don’t. But I think you want it.” Saint met her eyes then, and for the first time, he looked tired. Not physically — something deeper. Soul-tired.


“I’ve never wanted anything I could keep,” he said. “That’s the problem.”


Her breath caught. Silence stretched between them, thick and raw. “I didn’t call him,” she whispered. Saint blinked. Once. Then twice. “I thought about it,” she went on. “Thought it might be easier. Safer. But it didn’t feel right.”


He said nothing. Then, slowly, he set the gun down on the floor and stood. Not too close, but closer. “You scare me,” she admitted.


He let out a breath. “You scare me more.”


She stepped in then, heart pounding. One more inch and she’d be in his space. One more second and this would become real. He reached for her hand, not her waist, not her face, just her hand. Simple, reverent. She let him take it.


“I’ve done a lot of damage,” he said. “But I wouldn’t hurt you. Not even if you asked me to.”


Euphoria swallowed. Hard. Her free hand rose, slow and unsure, and touched the edge of the scar on his shoulder. Her fingers trembled slightly. “I don’t know what this is,” she whispered.


“I do,” he said.


And still—he didn’t kiss her. He just stood there, holding her hand like it was sacred, and for the first time since the night she found him bleeding in the dark, she wanted him to stay, not because he needed her, but because she needed him, too.
