Chapter Ten: The Phone Call


It happened on a Wednesday night. She’d come home from the hospital in her scrubs, ponytail loose, a little blood on her sleeve. She looked exhausted and beautiful in that way Saint was starting to find unbearable. He was at the stove, shirtless under that same hoodie, cooking something that smelled like garlic and butter and comfort. She raised a brow.


“You cook now?”


He shrugged. “I watched you make pasta once. Figured I’d give it a try.”


Euphoria smiled. “What, you kill for a living but can’t handle a colander?”


He smirked, handed her a fork, and she tasted it.


“Not bad,” she admitted.


“Not dead,” he countered. “Progress.”


Then her phone buzzed. She checked it without thinking, thumb swiping the screen. A man’s name. Saint didn’t recognize it. But the way she smiled—the quiet kind, the past kind—made something twist in his chest.


“Everything okay?” he asked, pretending to stir the sauce.


“Yeah. Just… an old friend. We used to date. He’s in town.”


He kept his voice even. “You gonna see him?”


She shrugged, a little too casually. “Maybe.” He said nothing. She could feel the temperature drop.


“Relax,” she said. “It’s not a big deal.”


“Didn’t say it was.”


But he didn’t touch the food again. Just leaned on the counter, arms crossed, eyes stormy.


Euphoria exhaled. “Are you seriously jealous right now?”


Saint laughed once—dry, sharp. “No. I’m just surprised you’re the kind of woman who calls exes back.”


Her eyes narrowed. “And you’re the kind of man who thinks everything in a woman’s life exists for his approval?”


He stepped toward her, close enough to taste the tension in the air. “I don’t approve,” he said, voice low. “But I feel it. And I don’t like it.” That caught her off guard.


“You think this is anything?” she asked, voice shaking just slightly. “You’re a ghost with a gun. I’m a trauma surgeon with a mortgage. This—whatever this is—ends. You know that, right?”


Saint stared at her for a long moment. Then nodded. “Yeah,” he said quietly. “I know.” He turned and walked away before she could say anything else. And when the food went cold, she didn’t touch it either.
