Chapter Eight: Close

Saint was healing faster now.


The fever had passed, the swelling had gone down, and his stitches were holding. He still moved like a man who didn’t trust his own body, but the pain wasn’t in control anymore.


Euphoria watched it all with a surgeon’s eye. But underneath her clinical detachment, something warmer had started to bloom.


She caught him doing small things. Washing his own dishes after meals.


Memorizing how she took her coffee—black, two sugars—and having it ready for her on the counter after her night shifts. Folding her laundry and stacking it neatly on the chair in her bedroom. Not just folded, but precisely, like every corner had to line up perfectly. Military folds.


“You’re dangerous with a Glock, but you fold towels like a housewife,” she said one morning, sipping her coffee.


Saint looked up from the pile of warm cotton in his lap. “I was trained to leave no trace. Folding helps me think.”


“You’re a weird man.”


“You let a weird man live on your couch.”


Touché.


That day passed quietly. Rain outside. Jazz on the record player. Euphoria reading on the couch, Saint fixing the busted cabinet door in her kitchen like he’d been there for years. Like they were a married couple who forgot how to argue.


That night, she woke to the sound of him screaming. Not loud, But tortured. Pained. She found him sitting up on the couch, drenched in sweat, breathing hard.


“Nightmare?” she asked gently, not turning on the light.


He didn’t look at her. Just nodded. She sat on the armrest beside him. Didn’t touch him. Just stayed close.


“You know what I hate most?” he said after a while.


“What?”


“I never feel human when I wake up. I feel... programmed. Like I’m still there. Still in it.”


Euphoria exhaled. “I used to have dreams about my mom. But in mine, I could save her. That’s worse.”


He looked at her then—really looked at her.


“You know I’ve been watching you, right?” he said.


Her brow lifted. “You’re subtle, but not invisible.”


Saint chuckled softly. “You walk like you’re too tired to bother with the world. But when you cut someone open, I bet you’re all steel.”


“I don’t like being watched.”


He leaned in just slightly, his voice low. “Then stop being so impossible to look away from.”


Her breath caught—but only for a second. Then she stood. “Get some rest.”


She walked back to her room without looking over her shoulder.She walked back to her room without looking over her shoulder. And she knew it.
