Chapter Twenty-Three: Ghost Protocol


It had been six days since Nicoli’s death. Six days of quiet.


Of sunlight slipping through trees, of soft coffee sips at dawn, of Euphoria stitching her own shoulder under Saint’s watchful eye — no fear in her fingers, no trembling in his. For the first time, they were beginning to believe it was over. That they’d made it out. Until the envelope arrived. No postmark.


Just her name scrawled in ink that looked rushed… familiar. Dr. Euphoria Thorne. Saint found it on the hood of their car before sunrise. He called her out to the porch, his eyes dark and cautious.


Euphoria opened it with steady fingers. Inside: a single photograph. Faded, taken through a cracked mirror. It showed a man in a hospital gown, bald, gaunt ,but unmistakable.


Saint stared at it, his entire body stilling. “No,” he whispered.


Euphoria’s hands trembled for the first time since the night they met. Because she knew that face too. And she had buried it. Literally.


Joaquin "Silk" Maddox. Her ex. Her first. The reason she left Atlanta. The reason she stopped believing in men with secrets.


A high-profile arms dealer with a smile that made liars into lovers — who had died on her OR table four years ago after a botched FBI sting. Except now… he was very much alive.


On the back of the photo were five words, scrawled in black marker:


“Did you really think I’d die?”


Saint’s eyes locked onto hers,"You knew him?"


“I loved him,” she said. “And I thought I killed him.”


Saint’s jaw flexed. “He’s not just your past. He’s our problem now.”


Euphoria closed her eyes for one breath. Then two. When she opened them, there was fire in her gaze. “Then let’s finish what I started.”
