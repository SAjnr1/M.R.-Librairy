Chapter Twenty-Five: Scalpel or Saint


They met her at dawn. Two black cars, clean suits, federal smiles. Euphoria and Saint had just finished fixing up the safehouse — a cabin tucked in the hills of Vermont, surrounded by trees that felt like silence and healing.


She stepped out in jeans and a tank top, hair in a long white braid, expecting another ghost. Instead, she saw a woman in navy — glasses, briefcase, no nonsense. “Dr. Thorne,” the woman said. “We’ve reviewed your case.”


Euphoria blinked. “My license was revoked.”


“Temporarily suspended,” the woman corrected. “Due to falsified identities and potential obstruction during the Maddox case. But thanks to the file you provided — and testimony from certain buried agencies — you’ve been cleared.”


A folder was handed to her. Inside: a reinstatement form. Signed. Sealed. All it needed was her name.


“You’re being offered your surgical position back,” the woman continued. “Full privileges. Clean slate. New life.”


Euphoria’s throat tightened. It was everything she’d worked for. Everything she’d sacrificed. Everything she thought she lost. But the woman wasn’t done, “There is, however, one condition.”


Saint stepped forward slightly, shoulders tensing.


The woman didn’t flinch, “You’ll be relocated to Boston Memorial. Full government protection. But we cannot allow known high-risk assets to remain in close proximity. Especially not one with Saint’s background.”


Euphoria stared, “You’re saying I can have my life back — but only if I leave him.”


“Exactly.”


Saint’s face was unreadable.


But his eyes... They pleaded silently. Not for her to stay. But for her to choose what she wanted. She looked down at the folder. Then at her hands. Hands that had healed dozens.


Hands that had pulled him off the pavement. Hands that had held both scalpel... and gun. She remembered the way he looked at her after Silk died. Like she was the miracle. Not the other way around.


And in that moment, she knew:


She hadn’t lost her life. She’d found it. Just not the way she thought. Euphoria closed the folder. And handed it back. "No"


The woman’s brow arched. “Excuse me?”


“I don’t want a clean slate. I want a true one. And it starts with him,” She turned to Saint.“I don’t need a title to save lives. I just need myself. And I only found her... when I found you.”


Saint’s eyes burned — soft, awed, undone. The agents didn’t argue. They just left. And the cabin was quiet again.


This time, for good. Euphoria exhaled slowly, stepping into Saint’s arms, her head against his chest. He didn’t say a word. He didn’t have to. She had chosen him, not over medicine, but through it. Because love wasn’t the end of her story.


It was the beginning of the life she finally got to write on her own terms.
